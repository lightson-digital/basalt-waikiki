## Booking Edge, Basalt Waikiki (paste into your AI project memory)

*Pre-Demo Backup, 2026-05-11*

**Rating snapshot:**
- OpenTable: 4.7 of 5, 2,218+ reviews. Diners' Choice.
- TripAdvisor: 4.4 of 5, 445 reviews. #19 of 1,749 Honolulu restaurants.
- Yelp: 6,407 photos, 2,026 reviews (rating blocked from direct scrape, count via search snippet).
- Google: Listing active, ratings not pulled directly.

**Top 6 review themes:**
1. Charcoal Buttermilk Pancakes are the single most-named dish, 9 of 20 sampled, positive.
2. Happy hour (2pm to 5pm at the bar) is repeatedly called "the best value in Waikiki," 6 of 20 sampled, positive.
3. Service is warm and named (servers Ronald, Jane, bartender Brittney called out), 7 of 20 sampled, positive.
4. The food-hall location is hard to find on first visit, 4 of 20 sampled, mixed.
5. Atmosphere reads as noisy and inconsistent versus the food quality, 3 of 20 sampled, mixed.
6. Takeout and weekday execution misses (cold coffee, wrong pancake count, overcooked poached egg), 4 of 20 sampled, negative.

**Top 3 FAQ gaps on the website:**
1. Did the charcoal pancakes survive the 2025 menu redesign. They did, but the homepage never says so. Press coverage confirms Chef Kosaka "changed mostly everything except the pancakes," and the operator's silence forces guests to find that from a third-party blog or a 2018 review.
2. How do I actually find the restaurant inside Dukes Lane Market & Eatery. Multiple reviewers say the entrance is easy to miss; the contact page gives an address, not a wayfinding line.
3. Does the 20% kamaʻāina discount apply at happy hour, at dinner, on takeout, or only on breakfast. The homepage carousel surfaces the discount as a tile but does not state the redemption rule.

**Top comp-parity gaps:**
- Roy's Waikiki publishes 4.7 of 5 with 6,797 OpenTable reviews and 3,569 TripAdvisor reviews. Basalt sits at 4.7 OpenTable with 2,218 and 4.4 TripAdvisor with 445. Basalt wins on ranking (#19 vs Roy's #73) but loses on volume, and the homepage does not surface the rank.
- Goofy Cafe & Dine publishes 4.5 of 5 on TripAdvisor with 1,811 reviews and 3,886 on Yelp. Goofy owns the "locavore breakfast in Waikiki" search default; Basalt owns the chef pedigree but does not surface it on the booking moment.
- MW Restaurant (Wade Ueoka, ex-Alan Wong, the same lineage as Lance Kosaka) publishes 3,525 OpenTable reviews and stakes a clear "chef-driven new American" position. Basalt has the same chef pedigree and a Waikiki location advantage; the homepage hides both behind a single "Reservations" button.

**3 fixes (ranked):**
1. Put a one-liner directly above the "Make a Reservation" button on the homepage: "Charcoal Buttermilk Pancakes still on the menu. Chef Lance Kosaka (17 years with Alan Wong) reframed everything else." This answers the dominant pre-booking anxiety (did the famous pancake survive the chef change) and surfaces the chef pedigree in the same breath. Copy change, ship today.
2. Add a wayfinding line under the address on Contact + homepage hero: "Inside Dukes Lane Market & Eatery, ground floor of Hyatt Centric Waikiki. Past the gelato counter, toward the back of the market." Reviewers and bloggers already write this; the operator should own it before the first-time guest walks past the entrance and books somewhere else.
3. Spell out the kamaʻāina rule on the carousel tile and on the reservation button hover: "20% off for kamaʻāina with valid Hawaiʻi ID. Breakfast, lunch, dinner. Happy hour excluded." This turns a generic promo tile into a redemption-ready offer, and it pre-empts the most common dinner-check anxiety ($60 night or $200 night) before the OpenTable click.
