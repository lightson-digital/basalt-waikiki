Post-experience touchpoint audit

No
No newsletter capture visible on the homepage. No first-visit incentive form, no "join the list for chef's specials." OpenTable captures email at reservation, but Basalt doesn't own that list as a first-party marketing channel.
Repeat or referral program
Yes, partial
20% kamaʻāina discount (launched July 2025, Hawaiʻi State ID required) is the only structured repeat lever. Gift cards exist as a top-nav link but route off-site through a third-party platform. No referral code, no birthday club, no returning-guest punch.
Active blog or newsletter
No
"Media" page lists ~60 press features through 2025, but no Basalt-authored content cadence. No monthly menu-change announcement, no chef's-table series email, no seasonal program visible.
Post-meal email sequence
No
No visible post-meal sequence in Basalt's voice. OpenTable sends a generic "rate your visit" prompt, but Basalt doesn't ride that touchpoint. The most-loyal moment (right after the charcoal pancakes) goes unused.

No
Top-nav "Gift Card" link routes off-site through a third-party platform. The hotel-walk-in audience is buying gift cards for friends visiting Oʻahu, and Basalt is paying a third-party cut on every redemption instead of capturing it direct.

Not verified
Treat as a free repeat hook. A weekly GBP post (this week's daily special, a Lance Kosaka brunch plate, a happy-hour pour) costs nothing and keeps Basalt in top-of-mind for past guests and hotel walk-ins checking the listing.

Three reactivation email starters
Menu redesign — chef-driven reactivation
The pancakes are still there. The rest of the menu isn't.
Lance Kosaka changed mostly everything except the charcoal pancakes when he took the kitchen in April 2025. Salmon chazuke risotto, guava baby back ribs, pork hash loco moco, citrus-cured salmon toast. If the last time you ate at Basalt was before the redesign, the room is the same and almost nothing else is.
→ See the new menu and book on OpenTable
Different daypart cross-sell — happy hour
You came for brunch. The happy hour is the other half of the room.
Most of the people who do brunch at Basalt tell us afterward they didn't know the bar runs a real 2-to-5 happy hour, not a tourist-priced one. Walk in for a drink and the burger Frolic Hawaii called "the best happy hour burger in Waikiki." No second reservation, just the bar.
→ See the happy hour menu
Kamaʻāina come-back
Hawaiʻi residents: we built the 20% for you.
The 20% kamaʻāina discount was the first one Basalt has ever run, and it's still on. Show a Hawaiʻi State ID at the table. The pancakes are still on the menu, the prime rib's still on the menu, and Lance Kosaka (Iolani, UH, 17 years at Alan Wong) is the one cooking. Built for the kamaʻāina table.
→ Book a kamaʻāina table on OpenTable
Three referral mechanics to ship
First-party gift card SKU on basaltwaikiki.com
The current top-nav gift card routes through a third-party platform. Bring it onto basaltwaikiki.com directly with a "send a Basalt night to a friend visiting Oʻahu" frame. Hotel-walk-in guests and kamaʻāina buy them for visiting friends and family year-round. Captures the redemption without paying a third-party cut, and the email lives on the Basalt list, not someone else's.
EffortLow RevenueMid
Charcoal-pancake photo post-meal SMS with friend code
After breakfast, table-side QR or follow-up SMS sends the guest their charcoal-pancake photo plus a one-time friend code (free dessert or a happy-hour drink on the friend's first visit). Catches them at peak enthusiasm, with the dish that's already on their camera roll, and the code is the easy thing to text to a friend they wish was at the table.
EffortLow RevenueMid
Kamaʻāina-Plus extension, +1 friend at the resident rate
Returning kamaʻāina who book a second visit bring one mainland friend at the 20% rate, no Hawaiʻi ID required for the +1. Compounds the discount already running. Turns local repeat into a local-plus-visitor mix and fills the off-peak Thursday and Sunday dinner slots most likely to need the help. The kamaʻāina is the seed, the friend is the upside.

Three repeat-visit ideas you can ship this week
Add a homepage email capture with a real reason ("first to know about Lance Kosaka's chef's-table nights and seasonal menu drops"). Owns the list direct, no OpenTable middleman, gives the operator a channel to ride the press cadence the brand already earns.
Low effort
Restart GBP weekly posts: Monday, this week's daily special, a brunch plate, or a happy-hour pour. Free distribution, surfaces directly to anyone searching "Basalt Waikiki" or "breakfast Waikiki" on Google, and the most-skipped repeat hook for chef-driven restaurants inside a hotel.
Low effort
Send a same-day post-meal email in Basalt's voice (not OpenTable's). One ask, a Google or OpenTable review, with the charcoal-pancake photo or the table photo attached. Owns the most-loyal moment instead of letting the third-party platform have it.
