## Repeat Hooks, Basalt Waikiki (paste into your AI project memory)

*First Draft · 2026-05-11*

**Touchpoint audit (current state):**
- Email signup: Not visible on homepage. No newsletter capture, no "join the list for chef's specials," no first-visit incentive form. The OpenTable handoff captures email for reservations, but Basalt doesn't own that list as a first-party marketing channel.
- Repeat / referral program: Partial. The 20% kamaʻāina discount (launched July 2025, Hawaiʻi State ID required) is the only structured repeat lever. No gift card SKU on the site (gift cards exist as a top-nav link but route off-site). No referral code, no birthday club, no "third visit on us" punch.
- Active blog or newsletter: No. The "Media" page lists ~60 press features and last shows 2025 activity, but no Basalt-authored content cadence, no chef's-table series email, no monthly menu-change announcement.
- GBP posts in last 30 days: Not verified. Treat as a free repeat hook the team can ship this week — weekly post on this week's daily special, a happy hour pour, or a Lance Kosaka brunch plate.
- Post-meal email: No visible sequence. OpenTable sends its generic "rate your visit" prompt, but Basalt doesn't ride that touchpoint with anything in its own voice.

**Lifecycle drip plan (4 touchpoints):**
- Day 1: Mahalo plus a 30-second nudge to share the charcoal-pancake photo or leave a Google line. Earns the right to send anything else later. Lightweight, ʻohana voice, no upsell.
- Week 1: Daypart cross-sell. If they came for breakfast, sell happy hour or the new dinner menu. If they came for dinner, sell brunch and the charcoal pancakes. Four dayparts under one roof is the structural moat — the drip mines it.
- Month 3: Kamaʻāina come-back for local diners (20% discount reminder, "we built this for you") and a special-occasion anchor for visitors (birthday plate, anniversary prime rib, the salmon chazuke risotto on a return trip).
- Season 2: Chef-led event. A Lance Kosaka chef's-table night, a guava-baby-back-rib seasonal return, a holiday tasting menu, or a private-dining offer for end-of-year groups. Earns press and email opens at the same time.

**3 reactivation email subjects (in Basalt's voice):**
1. The pancakes are still there. The rest of the menu isn't.
2. You came for brunch. The happy hour is the other half of the room.
3. Hawaiʻi residents: we built the 20% for you.

**3 referral mechanics (effort × revenue):**
1. Gift card SKU on the site (first-party, not third-party), Low effort × Mid revenue. The top-nav gift card currently routes off-site through a third-party platform. Bringing it onto basaltwaikiki.com with a "send a Basalt night to a friend visiting Oʻahu" frame turns a happy guest into an unpaid sales rep, with no OpenTable commission on the redemption.
2. Charcoal-pancake photo post-meal SMS with a friend code, Low effort × Mid revenue. After breakfast, table-side QR or follow-up SMS sends the guest their pancake photo plus a one-time friend code (free dessert or a happy-hour drink on the friend's first visit). Catches them at peak enthusiasm with the dish that's already on their camera roll.
3. Kamaʻāina-Plus extension, +1 friend at the resident rate, Mid effort × Mid revenue. Returning kamaʻāina who book a second visit bring one mainland friend at the 20% rate, no Hawaiʻi ID required for the +1. Compounds the kamaʻāina program already running, turns local repeat into a local-plus-visitor mix, fills the off-peak Thursday and Sunday dinner slots most likely to need help.
