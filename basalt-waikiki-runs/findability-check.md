### Schema check (verbatim from the static HTML)

- **Homepage:** One JSON-LD graph (Yoast plugin output). Contains: `WebPage`, `WebSite` + `SearchAction`, `BreadcrumbList`, `ImageObject`. No `Restaurant`, no `FoodEstablishment`, no `LocalBusiness`, no `Menu`, no `FAQPage`, no `Review`, no `AggregateRating`, no `Person` (Chef Kosaka), no `Offer`.
- **/menus:** Same Yoast defaults. The menus themselves are PDF links (breakfast, lunch, dinner, happy hour, wine, dessert, kids, dine-in vs takeout variants). No HTML menu text, no Menu schema, no prices in the page DOM.
- **/contact:** Yoast defaults only. Hours and address are visible in body text but not in schema. Note: hours block has a typo on the live site ("Lunch: 11:00pm - 1:00pm" should be "11:00am - 1:00pm"). The typo is in HTML, not schema, so AIs that read the visible page may serve incorrect lunch hours.
- **Missing for an LLM-citable Waikiki sit-down restaurant:** `Restaurant` on the homepage with address, geo, openingHoursSpecification, priceRange, servesCuisine, acceptsReservations, hasMenu. `Menu` on /menus with `MenuSection` for breakfast / lunch / happy hour / dinner and `MenuItem` for the signature dishes (Charcoal Buttermilk Pancakes, Prime Rib, Salmon Chazuke Risotto, Guava Baby Back Ribs). `FAQPage` covering the four real traveler anxieties: closed Tue/Wed, dinner check average, happy hour pricing, walk-in vs reservation. `Person` for Chef Lance Kosaka with sameAs to the Alan Wong, Mariposa, 53 By The Sea press archive. `Offer` for the 20% kamaʻāina discount and the 2pm to 5pm happy hour.

### Hawaiʻi-specific gap

The brand owns the editorial layer in Hawaiʻi (eight straight years across Honolulu Magazine, Hawaii News Now, Star-Advertiser, Frolic Hawaii, KHON, Forbes, Food & Wine, TBS Japan) but the discovery layer (the listicle-tier "best of Waikiki" pages that AI synthesis leans on for non-branded prompts) is owned by competitors. On the branded query "Basalt Waikiki," OpenTable and TripAdvisor rank above the operator's homepage. On the category prompts (best breakfast, best happy hour, where locals eat) Basalt is either bonus-tier or absent from the listicles. The fastest fix is structural: Restaurant schema, HTML menus (PDFs are blocked from crawlers by the site's own robots.txt), and a homepage title rewrite that puts pancakes + Lance Kosaka + happy hour + kamaʻāina in the snippet. None of those require new press; they just stop hiding what eight years of press already built.
