3 fixes, ranked by impact

01

Add Restaurant JSON-LD to the homepage with address, hours, price range, cuisine, and menu reference

Today the only schema you publish is Yoast's WebPage and WebSite defaults. An AI answering "best breakfast spot in Waikiki," "where do locals eat in Waikiki," or the branded query has no machine-readable record that you are a Restaurant, that you serve breakfast through dinner, that you are closed Tue/Wed, that the price range is $$ to $$$, or that the cuisine is New American with Hawaiʻi-regional accents. Restaurant schema with openingHoursSpecification, priceRange, servesCuisine, acceptsReservations, and hasMenu is the one fix that closes three prompts at once.

Where: Homepage `<head>`. Mirror the address, geo, telephone, and hours from /contact (fix the "Lunch: 11:00pm" typo in the visible HTML while you are in there).

Effort Low Impact High
02

Move every menu off PDF onto HTML pages, then unblock them in robots.txt

Your robots.txt blocks `*.pdf$`, which means GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended cannot read the breakfast, lunch, dinner, happy hour, wine, dessert, or kids menus. An AI answering "where can I get great pancakes in Honolulu" can quote Honolulu Magazine's review of the charcoal pancake but cannot quote your actual dish name, price, or description because the source file is hidden behind a disallow rule. HTML menus with Menu schema (`MenuSection` per daypart, `MenuItem` per dish, `Offer` per price) make every dish quotable by name.

Where: /menus replaces PDF links with HTML pages per daypart (breakfast, lunch, happy hour, dinner). Add `Menu` schema. Remove `Disallow: /*.pdf$` from robots.txt, or leave it and rely on the HTML versions as the canonical source.

Effort Mid Impact High
03

Rewrite the homepage title tag and meta description to lead with pancakes, Kosaka, happy hour, and kamaʻāina

Current title is "Basalt Waikiki Restaurant - Inside Dukes Lane Market And Eatery." It buries the four things eight years of press has taught travelers to associate with the brand. Rewrite to: "Basalt Waikiki · Charcoal Pancakes, Happy Hour, Dinner by Chef Lance Kosaka." New meta: "Sit-down breakfast, brunch, and dinner in Waikiki. Famous charcoal buttermilk pancakes, 2pm to 5pm happy hour, modern New American by Chef Lance Kosaka (17 years with Alan Wong). 20% kamaʻāina discount. Inside Dukes Lane Market & Eatery." Closes the breakfast prompt, the locals-eat-here prompt, and the happy hour prompt by putting the brand's actual proof points in the snippet AI Overviews extract.

Where: Homepage title + meta description (Yoast SEO settings). Apply the same pattern to /menus, /in-the-kitchen, /lance-kosaka, and /contact.

Effort Low Impact High
